About Me

Hello! I am a graduate student in the Computational Science, Engineering, and Mathematics (CSEM) PhD program at the Oden Institute at UT Austin. I work with George Biros, where I am developing fast surrogates for solving chemical networks with error guarantees. I am also a member of the NSF-Simons AI Institute for Cosmic Origins (CosmicAI). My research interests focus on numerical analysis, applied mathematics, scientific computing, and developing/analyzing algorithms for solving differential equations.

Prior to joining Oden, I completed my undergraduate work at the Colorado School of Mines, where I graduated summa cum laude with a B.S. in Computational and Applied Mathematics and a minor in Physics. I did undergraduate research with Steve Pankavich, where I worked on using active subspace methods to analyze sensitivities of plasmas arising in tokamaks and stellarators.

Research

HATANN: High-dimensional approximation using Taylor expansions and Adaptive Nearest Neighbors

Including chemistry to MHD in large-scale star simulations increases computational costs by around 10x. Hence, we are interested in creating a fast surrogate to replace the full chemistry solve. HATANN is an algorithm that uses nearest neighbors to achieve this, along with Taylor expansions to include error guarantees.
